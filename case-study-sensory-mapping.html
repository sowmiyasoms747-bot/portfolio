Sensory-Friendly Routing

Shifting navigation from a speed-focused system to a comfort-driven experience, prioritizing predictability, accessibility, and emotional well-being throughout the journey.

01

Entry Point (Sensory Toggle)

A new “Sensory” option is introduced directly within the directions interface of Google Maps. Placing the feature here ensures discoverability at the moment users are actively planning a journey, rather than hiding it inside settings.

02

Sensory Preferences

Users can quickly personalize their experience by selecting sensitivity levels for noise, crowd density, accessibility, and rest stops. The design keeps interactions simple and optional, reducing cognitive load while still allowing meaningful customization.

03

Route Selection (Sensory Scoring)

Routes are presented with a sensory score alongside time and distance, allowing users to balance comfort and efficiency. Instead of labeling routes, a neutral scoring system makes the feature inclusive and accessible to all users.

04

Map with Environmental Overlays

The map view is enhanced with visual overlays indicating quiet zones, busy areas, and available rest points. This helps users build a mental model of their journey and reduces uncertainty before and during navigation.

05

Navigation with Smart Alerts

During navigation, the system provides proactive alerts about upcoming crowded or noisy areas. Users are offered optional detours, ensuring they remain in control while being supported by real-time insights.

06

Post-Trip Feedback

After completing the journey, users can quickly share their experience using simple feedback options. This lightweight interaction helps improve route recommendations over time without interrupting the user’s flow.

07

AI Learning (Implicit Experience)

The system learns from user preferences and feedback across trips, gradually reducing the need for repeated inputs. Over time, the experience becomes more personalized, predictive, and effortless.
