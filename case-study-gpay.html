Overview

What is GPay's Today's Spend Indicator?

This project adds a Today's Spend Indicator feature to Google Pay. It shows the running total of money spent since midnight directly on the payment screen — right above the amount field — so users always know how much they have already spent that day before making another payment. The indicator updates automatically after every transaction and resets itself every midnight without any setup or manual input from the user.

Why I Chose This

As a regular GPay user, I experienced paying blindly throughout the day and only realizing I was running short on cash when it was too late. I wanted to design an elegant solution that creates financial awareness in the exact moment of transaction, without adding friction.

The Goal

Provide immediate, zero-friction visibility of daily spending at the moment of payment, helping users stay conscious of their budget without the chore of manual tracking or complex setups.

User Research

Research & Validation

Validating the awareness gap with real users and reviewing the existing GPay structure.

👥

User Interviews

I spoke with 5 regular GPay users and asked a simple question: "Do you know how much you have spent today right now?"

  • None of them could answer without opening their transaction history.
  • 3 out of 5 had experienced running out of money unexpectedly because they lost track.
🔍

Interface Analysis

Examining GPay's information architecture and flow:

  • Transaction history exists but requires navigating to a separate screen.
  • No summary of daily spending is visible during the active payment flow.
  • Account balance tells you what is left, but not how much you have already spent.
💡

Key Finding

The spending data already exists inside the app. No new data collection or complex systems are needed — we simply need to place that information at the exact moment of decision-making.

Key Insights

Insights from the Field

⚡

Awareness in the Moment

Awareness at the moment of action is infinitely more powerful than after-the-fact reflection. Knowing your total spend just before you pay changes behavior; knowing it at the end of the day changes nothing.

👁️

Awareness over Budgeting

Users don't want to budget — they want to be aware. Budgeting requires setup, discipline, and planning. Awareness requires zero effort if the right information is presented at the right time.

⏳

Transaction History is Too Far

Buried deep in separate pages, transaction logs require mental calculation and navigation. By the time a user digs into it, the decision to spend has already been made.

🔄

Midnight Auto-Reset

Automatic daily resets remove friction completely. If users had to manually reset a counter, it would be forgotten. The auto-reset ensures a fresh start every single day.

The Strategy

Zero-Friction Design Decisions

To keep GPay's fast, tap-and-go experience intact, I based the design on four key architectural decisions focused on simplicity and awareness.

Decision 1: Zero Setup Required

No budgets to configure or daily limits to set. The tracker is useful from the very first payment, for every type of user, with absolutely zero configuration friction.

Decision 2: Context-Aware Placement

Placed directly above the amount field, which is where the user's focus naturally goes before entering a payment. No extra navigation, no extra taps.

Decision 3: Informational, Not Judgmental

The indicator presents a clean number with no judgmental colors or limit bars. It trusts the user's judgement, informing them without making them feel restricted.

Decision 4: Automatic Maintenance

The daily spend total is calculated dynamically and resets automatically at midnight. The user never has to maintain or think about the tool; it just works.

Reflection

What I Learned

01

Simple is Powerful

The best UX solutions are not always the most complex ones. A simple running total placed in the right spot is more effective than full budgeting dashboards that users rarely visit.

02

Reposition Existing Data

GPay already had the transaction logs. UX design is often about relocating existing data to a moment where it can actively support user behavior.

03

Lived Experience is Research

Experiencing the frustration myself drove a focused, honest solution. Personal habits can be a powerful launchpad for identifying universal usability issues.
